# ApricotPlayer v0.9.45

Fixes a stream format regression introduced during the modular refactor that caused seeking to be broken or slow and RAM usage to be much higher than expected.

## Bug fixes

- **Fixed broken/slow seeking and high RAM usage on YouTube streams.** The stream URL resolver was using `best[ext=mp4]/best` as the yt-dlp format selector. For YouTube Music tracks, very long videos, and content where no combined progressive MP4 exists, this fell back to a DASH segment URL — a URL pointing to only a few seconds of audio. mpv received the segment, played it, and stopped. Seeking had no backward buffer and no range-request support, causing the "cut off from front/back" symptom reported by users. Because combined 720p video+audio (format 22) was often selected even for audio-only playback, the demuxer cache was filling with video data and mpv was initialising a full video pipeline (~300 MB RAM) even with `--vid=no`. The format selector is now `bestaudio[ext=m4a][protocol=https]/bestaudio[protocol=https]/best[ext=mp4][protocol=https]/best[acodec!=none][vcodec!=none][protocol=https]/best[ext=mp4]/best[acodec!=none][vcodec!=none]/best`. The `[protocol=https]` filter ensures yt-dlp returns a whole-file HTTP URL (range-requestable, seekable) rather than a DASH segment. Audio-only m4a is prioritised: it is ~30x smaller than a combined video+audio stream, fills the cache in seconds rather than minutes, allows instant seeking anywhere in the track, and keeps mpv's RAM at ~30–80 MB instead of ~300+ MB.
